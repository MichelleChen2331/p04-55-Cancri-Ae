Example Data #2: Exoplanet Discovery Methods

This pie chart shows the distribution of how exoplanets were discovered. Go to the definitions page for an explanation of each discovery method. Click on one of the colored boxes to show/hide any discovery method.

The distribution of discovery methods is noticably heavy in the transit method. This is the result of multiple factors: variations in spectra from transits are easiest to observe AND confirm, do not require planets to be orbitting in a head-on axis, and are immediately obvious in data rather than requiring a dedicated search.






Data sourced from NASA Exoplanet Archive, website by team 55 Cancri-Ae
