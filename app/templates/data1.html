Example Data #1: Exoplanet Discoveries Per Year

This graph shows the number of exoplanets discovered each year, colored by count. Hover over any one bar to see the year and exact count.

In 2014, the most exoplanets were "discovered" because NASA's Kepler Space Telescope announced many at once. Recent advancements had been made in detection, and a huge release in data resulted in the surprisingly sudden uptick in discoveries in 2014.







Data sourced from NASA Exoplanet Archive, website by team 55 Cancri-Ae
